About DUCK

DUCK (Dataverse Unified Cross-modal Knowledge-grounding) is a large-scale benchmark for evaluating the ability of language models to ground natural language questions over heterogeneous enterprise data.

Unlike existing text-to-SQL benchmarks that focus on clean relational tables, DUCK challenges models to reason across the full spectrum of data modalities found in real-world enterprise environments — CRM records, system logs, file attachments, multi-line text fields, and meeting transcripts.

Data Modalities

Large-Scale Relational Data

Hundreds of interconnected tables with complex foreign key relationships, polymorphic joins, and hierarchical entity structures.

CRM Data

Accounts, contacts, leads, opportunities, and cases with domain-specific business semantics.

Business Logic

Tables encoding business rules, status transitions, approval workflows, and computed fields.

File Attachments

Queries requiring reasoning over metadata and content of files stored as annotations or attachments.

System & Audit Logs

Structured log data capturing user actions, system events, and change history.

Multi-Line Text Fields

Rich-text and multi-line fields (notes, descriptions, email bodies) requiring text parsing within SQL context.

Meeting Recaps & Transcripts

Semi-structured meeting summaries and transcripts linked to CRM entities.
